Alpine Pass Weekend — "High‑Alpine Rush"

Experience an unforgettable journey through the heart of Central Europe, where medieval towns meet towering Alpine peaks and every mile brings a new breathtaking view. This compact but adventure-packed route leads you from Prague into the Austrian Alps, weaving through sparkling lakes, dramatic valleys, and some of the continent’s most iconic high mountain passes. With smooth tarmac, twisty serpentine roads, and legendary viewpoints like Edelweißspitze, this weekend escape is designed for riders who crave scenery, variety, and the thrill of climbing into the clouds. From the charm of Český Krumlov to the sweeping panoramas of the Grossglockner High Alpine Road and the vibrant atmosphere of Innsbruck, the trip blends cultural highlights with world-class riding before looping back toward Czechia through peaceful border towns. Perfect for anyone seeking a concentrated dose of Alpine magic in just a few days.

Trip details

  • Distance: 1,000 to 1,200 km
  • Countries: Czechia, Austria
  • Type: Sightseeing / Mountain Touring
  • Estimated days: 4
  • Suitable for: Riders who enjoy twisty roads, scenic passes, and short cultural stops

Roadmap

  1. Prague — Departure
  2. Český Krumlov — Quick town visit, refuel
  3. Linz — Cross into Austria
  4. Salzburg — Short city stop
  5. Bischofshofen → Zell am See — Lakes and mountain views
  6. Heiligenblut — Start of Grossglockner ascent
  7. Edelweißspitze — High Alpine viewpoint
  8. Lienz — Descent into East Tyrol
  9. Innsbruck — Optional Tyrolean pass loop
  10. Kufstein — Northbound toward Czech border
  11. Return to Prague
